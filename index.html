About the Hobby

WHAT IS
DIECAST?

Diecast collectibles are precision-made metal model cars manufactured by pouring molten metal into a die — a steel mould — under pressure. The result is a highly detailed, durable miniature replica of real-world vehicles, from everyday street cars to rare supercars and race machines.

Hot Wheels, launched by Mattel in 1968, is the world's leading diecast brand — known for bold liveries, fantasy castings, and premium Real Riders lines. Matchbox focuses on realism and scale accuracy. Together they've shaped a global community of millions of collectors.
